Season Overview

Five Seasons.
One Bright Spot.

Since entering the league in 1970, the Vancouver Canucks have been a streaky team. Periods of strong performance are often followed by sharp downturns and the past five seasons reflect that pattern precisely.

Across five different team iterations, the Canucks made the playoffs just once. That was in 2024, when they posted the best record in their division at 50-23-9, earning 109 standings points.

Two years later, they find themselves dead last in the league with one of the worst seasons in franchise history.

Methodology

Our Approach

We focused on keeping the data consistent and comparable across seasons. Each NHL team plays 82 games in the regular season, so we used regular season data only. This allowed us to fairly compare performance year over year without the variability of playoff games.

Player Profile

The Next
Captain?

In early 2026, the Canucks traded away their captain Quinn Hughes in a blockbuster deal to Minnesota. Ever since his departure, fans and analysts have been speculating about who will wear the C on their chest next.

A name that keeps surfacing is Elias Pettersson the Swedish forward drafted 5th overall in 2017. He won the Calder Trophy in his rookie season and has since evolved into an NHL All-Star and the unquestioned centrepiece of Vancouver's offence.

Pettersson recently signed a long-term extension a statement to fans and the league that he is here for the long haul. Though hockey is a team sport, we expect our stars to shine and lead the way. So how has EP40 performed and what does it tell us about the Canucks' trajectory?

Conclusion

The Road
Ahead

The Canucks' performance over five seasons is shaped by three interlocking forces: concentrated offensive production, inconsistent team balance between offence and defence, and the outsized influence of their star player. Long term success will require addressing all three: broadening offensive depth, tightening defence, and ensuring Elias Pettersson is surrounded by a system designed to win.

Concentrated Scoring

Most goals are scored through a small group of players. That makes the team easy to defend against, and if those players get injured, the team suffers significantly.

Balance Wins Games

2023–24 proved it: elite offence paired with stingy defence is the winning formula. That combination has appeared exactly once in five years.

The Pettersson Effect

Pettersson's performance mirrors team fortune with striking consistency. His health, form, and eventual captaincy are crucial to any future success making his long term commitment all the more significant.
